Our Story

Born from a passion
for the table

In 1987, chef Emilio Conti opened La Volta in a vaulted cellar on Via dei Servi — a space that had once stored Florentine wine barrels. The arched stone ceilings gave the restaurant its name, and its soul.

Today, his daughter Sofia leads the kitchen, honoring recipes passed down through three generations while weaving in the quiet innovations of her own craft. Every dish is an argument for slowness — for the braise, the hand-rolled pasta, the sauce that simmers all morning.

We source from the same farms our family has visited for decades. We change the menu when the seasons do, and not a moment before.

Why La Volta

The full experience

🍷

Cellar & Wine

Our sommelier curates over 200 labels from Tuscany, Piedmont, and beyond. Each pairing is chosen to honour the dish, not compete with it. Private cellar tastings available on request.

👨‍🍳

The Kitchen

Chef Sofia Conti trained in Lyon and Modena before returning to Florence. Her philosophy: seasonal, humble ingredients elevated through patience. The pasta is made every morning, by hand.

🏛️

The Space

Stone vaulted ceilings from the 14th century. Candlelight. Linen. No background music — just conversation. La Volta seats 42 guests; we believe intimacy cannot be scaled.
